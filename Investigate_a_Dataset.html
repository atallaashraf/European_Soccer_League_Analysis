Introduction¶

In this project the dataset European Soccer Database is selected for analysis. The dataset contains data on more than 25,000 games in 11 European countries. The database is explained in: https://www.kaggle.com/hugomathien/soccer and can be downloaded from: https://www.google.com/url?q=https://d17h27t6h515a5.cloudfront.net/topher/2017/November/5a0a4cad_database/database.sqlite&sa=D&ust=1532469042122000

The project starts with understanding that different table and collecting the columns of interest. The columns of interest are defined based on the type of analysis that will be used to compare and investigate the different leagues and teams.

Analysis Questions¶

Based on my preliminary investigation, given this database it could be interesting to look at the next questions:

League competitiveness¶

The increase in payouts to the European football clubs in each league drove a major question of how competitive the league is? The methodologies used when analyzing competitive balance are a subject of a wide discussion and is essential to estimate the worth of broadcasting and rights of each league.

Team performance¶

In this section, a team-focused study is performed, and the following questions are addressed:

  1. What are the top three most effective teams in each league?
  2. What are the top three most accomplished teams in Europe?
  3. What are the top ten teams in Europe collected the most points per game for all seasons?
  4. What are the top ten teams with the strongest attack/defense lines?

Separating team and league records¶

In order to assess the questions posed in this analysis. Each league's table has to be calculated using the league, team, and match data. The function get_team_records returns the team records for a given team id and a given dataframe.

Once each team's records are calculated, two variables are built:

  1. Nested dictionary: a dictionary (season_table) that holds the league and season data. It can be used for an easy access to the separated tables for each season/league.
  2. Full dataframe: a dataframe (season_table_full) that holds the teams records for all leagues and all seasons. It can be used to perform large scale comparisons between leagues or teams from different leagues. It is also useful to store the refined data to a csv file.

def get_team_records(team,df):
    '''
    Calculates the team records for a given the team and a given dataframe that holds the leagues matchs.
    Returns the team records : games, wins, draws, losses, goals scored. goals received, goal difference, points.
    '''
    # create an empty array to hold the team records
    team_records = np.empty([0,8],dtype=int)
    # get the team matches that are played at home and away
    team_home_matches = df.query('home_team_api_id == @team')
    team_away_matches = df.query('away_team_api_id == @team')
    # calculate the wine/draw/loss of the team when plays home or away
    home_win = (team_home_matches.home_team_goal > team_home_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    home_draw = (team_home_matches.home_team_goal == team_home_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    home_loss = (team_home_matches.home_team_goal < team_home_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    away_win = (team_away_matches.home_team_goal < team_away_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    away_draw = (team_away_matches.home_team_goal == team_away_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    away_loss = (team_away_matches.home_team_goal > team_away_matches.away_team_goal).sum()
    # calculate the total playes games
    total_home_games = np.sum([home_win,home_draw,home_loss])
    total_away_games = np.sum([away_win,away_draw,away_loss])
    # calculate the team scored goals
    scored = team_home_matches.home_team_goal.sum() + team_away_matches.away_team_goal.sum()
    # calculate the team received goals
    received = team_away_matches.home_team_goal.sum() + team_home_matches.away_team_goal.sum()
    
    # store data in the team_records array
    team_records = np.append(team_records,total_home_games + total_away_games) # total games
    team_records = np.append(team_records,home_win + away_win) # games won
    team_records = np.append(team_records,home_draw + away_draw) # games tied
    team_records = np.append(team_records,home_loss + away_loss) # games lost  
    team_records = np.append(team_records,scored) # scored goals
    team_records = np.append(team_records, received) # received goals
    team_records = np.append(team_records, scored-received) # goal difference                       
    team_records = np.append(team_records,3 * (home_win + away_win) + home_draw + away_draw) # total points
    
    return team_records.reshape((1, 8))

Exploratory Data Analysis¶

In this section, the questions proposed in the first sections are addressed and discussed.

League Competitiveness¶

To assess the league competitiveness, multiple indicators can be used such as[1]:

  1. The Hirshman Herfindahl Index $(HI)$
  2. The concentration ratio $(CR_i)$
  3. The standard deviation of league points $(SDLP)$
  4. The standard deviation of team points $(SDTP)$
  5. The competitive balance ratio $(CBR)$

Hirshman Herfindahl Index¶

Concept¶

Originally used in finance, the Hirshman Herfindahl Index (HI) is based on the sum of the quadratic share of points $s_i^2$ won by each club in a league with $N$ teams. To account for changes in the size of leagues over time, this measure is modified to the so-called H-Index of Competitive Balance (HICB), which is the ratio of the HI to the HI of a perfectly balanced league (Depken, 1999):

\begin{equation} HICB = \dfrac{\sum_1^N s_i^2}{1/N}\times 100 \end{equation}

Where $s_i$ is the percentage of the point share of each team to the total won points in the league.

\begin{equation} s_i = \dfrac{TP_i}{\sum_1^N TP_i} \end{equation}

Where $TP$ is the team points collected at the end of the league.

Results and Analysis¶

Looking into the results of the HICB, each league seems to vary in the competitiveness level from one season to another. Some leagues are more competitive in general while others are dominated. Additionally, by looking at the mean HICB for each league, we can see that the Polish league (Ekstraklasa) is the most competitive among the leagues while the Portuguese league (Liga ZON Sagres) is the most dominated.

Also, the figure below shows the most competitive season in each league. This can be found by calculating the season with the minimum HICB.

We can notice a decrease in the HICB in some leagues which is an indication of competitiveness increase. To compare the trend in competitiveness between leagues, the slope of the best-fit line is calculated. As shown below, some leagues have seen an improvement in competitiveness over time while others shown a decline in competitiveness that reflects an increase in dominance.

It is interesting to see that out of the top 4 rated leagues (English, Spanish, Italian, German), only the English Premier League (EPL) has improved! This can be justified by the distribution of league winners for each of the four leagues. Which is shown below. While the Spanish La Liga was dominated by nearly two teams, the EPL distribution shows more breadth when looking at the winners.

Proposed Method: Points to First Place¶

This is a proposed method to measure the competitiveness of a league. It is based on finding the mean number of points each team in a league of $N$ teams needs to reach the first place, as follows:

\begin{equation} C = \dfrac{\sum_2^N p_i-p_1}{N-1} \end{equation}

where $C$ is the normalized measure of competitiveness and $p_i$ is the points of the $i^{th}$ team.

This can be used to measure how far each team from the top team. It can also be used to compare a slice of teams or compare teams to each other. As shown below, this method provides a good measure of the competitiveness. It provides similar trend as the Hirshman Herfindahl Index with correlation coefficient of 0.363.

        s = season_table[league_id][season].pts/np.sum(season_table[league_id][season].pts)
        # calculate the HICB
        HICB[league_id].append(np.sum(s**2)*len(season_table[league_id][season])*100)
    
    # calculating the mean HICB for each league
    HICB_avg[league_id] = np.mean(HICB[league_id])
    
    # calculating the minimum HICB (most competitive season)for each league
    HICB_min[league_id] = np.min(HICB[league_id])
    HICB_best[league_id] = all_seasons[np.argmin(HICB[league_id])]
    
    # finish the plot
    i += 1
    ax = plt.subplot(4, 3, i)
    ax.bar(xpos, HICB[league_id],width=0.8)
    plt.xlabel('Season')
    plt.ylabel('HICB')
    title = df_league.loc[df_league['id'] == league_id].values[0][1]
    ax.set_title(title)
    plt.xticks(xpos, xticks, rotation=90, fontsize=8)

Team Analysis¶

In this section, the team data are analyzed to address the following questions:

  1. What are the top three most effective teams in each league?
  2. What are the top three most accomplished teams in Europe?
  3. What are the top three teams with the strongest attack/defense lines?

Most effective teams¶

In this investigation, a team is considered more effective if it wins more matches. The effectiveness of a team is therefore calculated as the mean points per match. An effective team will collect a higher mean points per match.

The following chart shows the top 10 teams with the highest average points per game. The top three are:

  1. FC Barcelona - 2.45 pts/game
  2. FC Porto - 2.38 pts/game
  3. SL Benfica - 2.38 pts/game

Most accomplished teams¶

In this investigation, a team is considered more accomplished if it wins more league trophies. The chart below shows the league champions across the European leagues in the period of 2008/2009 and 2015/2016.

The top three accomplished teams are:

  1. FC Basel - 7 league trophies
  2. FC Barcelona - 6 league trophies
  3. Celtic, RSC Anderlecht, Juventus, FC Bayern Munchin - 5 league trophies

Best offensive/defensive teams¶

In this investigation, the goodness of the offense is measured by the average goals scored per game. Similarily, the goodness of the defense is measured by the least number of goals attained per game.

The best three offensive teams are:

  1. FC Barcelona - 2.79 goals/game
  2. Real Madrid CF - 2.77 goals/game
  3. FC Bayern Munchin - 2.40 goals/game

The best three defensive teams are:

  1. FC Porto - 0.65 goals/game

Conclusions¶

In this project the dataset European Soccer Database was selected for analysis. The dataset contains data on more than 25,000 games in 11 European leagues. The analysis showed the following:

  • The Polish league is the most competitive league while the -Portuguese league is the least competitive.
  • Out of the top 5 leagues, the English Premier League is the only league that shows improvement in competitiveness in the period of 2008/2009 to 2015/2016.
  • FC Barcelona is the most effective team with 2.45 points per game. It is also the strongest offense with 2.79 goals per game.
  • FC Porto is the strongest defense with 0.65 attained goals per game. Both Scottish teams, Celtic and Rangers, come in the second and third position.
  • The swiss team FC Basel is the team with the most league trophies.

A new method of measuring the level of competitiveness is proposed. It showed a good correlation with the Hirshman Herfindahl Index and can be a good basis for further research.
